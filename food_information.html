Foods to Bring

We love to eat.

For a short hiking trip, it is usually better to consider carrying lighter foods that are still able to provide you with enough energy for the hike. Even if you do not think you will get hungry on your hike, it is best to carry some food as the additional exercise may make you hungrier than usual. Non-perishable foods are always your best bet, as they will be easier to keep fresh. Some of the easiest foods to bring for a short one-day hiking trip are:

  • - Trail mix (avoid chocolate mixes)
  • - Dried fruit
  • - Energy bars
  • - Beef jerky
  • - Peanut butter and jelly sandwiches

If you decide you want to go on a longer hiking trip over the weekend, the food you take will need to have a balance of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats. If your body does not receive the appropriate nutrients, your metabolic system will not perform as well. Your body will be strained from the lack of a proper diet, and you will be weakened making it more difficult to finish your hike and avoid cramps and soreness. In order to make your hiking trip more enjoyable, eating what your body craves is important. Some easy multi-day camping foods are listed below:

  • - Bread (you can substitute tortillas for ease of packing)
  • - Bananas
  • - Apples
  • - Peanut butter
  • - Breakfast cereal
  • - Crackers
  • - Squeezable apple sauce
  • - Poultry or fish pouches
  • - Canned tuna
  • - Freeze-dried meals

The items listed above are just a few of the many options available to you when you go hiking for a weekend. Bring a few of the snacks listed for taking a one-day hike refuel energy in the middle of a hike. Food preparation is an important step to having an enjoyable hiking experience for you and your friends.
