Staying Safe

Health Benefits of Hiking

If you don’t usually go to Eppley or if you want a change of pace, going outdoors and soaking up the fresh air while hiking might be the perfect exercise for you! Hiking is a moderate intensity aerobic activity that is easily accessible to college students at the University of Maryland. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that adults get at least 2 hours and 30 minutes every week of moderate intensity aerobic activity. Hiking, along with all physical activity, has been proven to prevent cardiovascular disease, reduce diagnoses of diabetes, and is often recommended as an aid in the treatment for heart disease. Incidences of obesity on college campuses have been increasing in part due to lack of physical activity as well as diet. Another consequence of inactivity is hypertension or high blood pressure, which can cause heart attacks, strokes, and other problems. Taking a little time away from classes to go to a nearby hiking trail can beneficial to your health in the long run and leave you feeling invigorated. If you are looking to burn a few calories, you can burn 200-250 calories an hour walking at a pace of 2.5 miles per hour, and even more if you go uphill or carry your backpack!

After a particularly tough exam, you will be hunting for a way to relax and relieve stress. According to a psychology article by Velarde et al, “Natural landscapes were found to have a more positive effect on health than urban landscapes”. While laying down on McKeldin can provide a quick relaxing space, going further away from the urban style of campus on a nature hike can positively affect your mental health. Hiking on trails near campus allows you as a student to escape the constant stress on campus and give your mind a psychological break.

Food Safety

Food safety is an important criterion whether you’re taking a short hiking trip or a month long hiking trip. Any food you take must be stored at the appropriate temperatures. If your food is originally refrigerated or frozen, try freezing water bottles as ice packs. Throughout your hike, the water bottles will keep your food cold and melt to provide you with more water later. Hand sanitizer is an important item to bring along when you are hiking if you are planning to handle food. Microorganisms and animal fecal matter can be present in any of the dirt your hands accumulate during your hike – so wash your hands! The water in the rivers and lakes surrounding you as you hike can also have dangerous substances within them. Even when the water appears crystal clear, it has the potential to put you in the hospital. Whenever you go hiking, make sure you bring either your own drinking water, instruments for boiling water, or water purification tablets. Food safety should not be your only concern, nutrition is also important for your hikes.
